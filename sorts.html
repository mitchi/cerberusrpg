Hide spell

Hide a spell’s aura so that it is not detected by a lesser wizard.

Wizard 3, Illusion

Counters : Blanket Dispel, Divination/Abjuration detection

Action: Standard action

Cast Range: close

Components: V,S

Duration: permanent

The Wizard casts Hide Spell to hide an ongoing spell effect from other wizards. Spell effects and auras can be detected very easily with the Detect Magic spell usually. However, when Hide Spell is used, a Detect Magic user won’t be able to detect the presence of the hidden spell unless he succeeds with a DC check.

Here is how this DC check is performed:

DC check = DC of detection spell vs DC of Hide Spell.

DC of detection spell = 1d20 - 10 + DC

Spell trap

Set up a spell as a trap on an object or surface

Wizard 4, Abjuration

Counters : Dispel

Action: Standard action in addition to casting the first spell

Cast Range: Close

Components: V,S,M (gold required to keep the spell in suspension)

Duration: Permanent until dispelled

The Wizard casts a spell on an object. The spell activates on a specific trigger programmed by the Wizard.

Spelltrap can hear, see and smell.

The spell can have a number of triggers and conditions equal to the INT modifier of the wizard.

For instance, a set of conditions can be : The creature is a man with red hair & the password has been pronounced in the last 10 seconds.

Keeping the spell in a permanent state requires a certain amount of pure gold. This amount is calculated in the following manner:

Amount of gold  = 5 gold * Spell Level * Spell Level * Caster Level.

Ennervation

Drain the power of a living creature with negative energy

Wizard 5, Necromancy (negative energy)

Counters: Spell Resistance, Abjuration barriers, Counterspell, Dodge

Action: Standard action

Target: close

Components: V,S,M (onyx or onyx dust)

Attack a living creature with negative energy. The attack is a ray of negative energy. The ray is a ranged touch attack that can be dodged. If the ray reaches critical areas of the body, it will do twice more damage (the touch attack can crit).

Magic power is converted into negative at a rate of 1 negative for 10 magic.

By supplying an appropriate material component to the spell, such as onyx dust, the conversion rate is twice as effective.

So a CL20 Wizard will produce 2 levels of negative energy. And the wizard with the Onyx will produce 4 levels of negative energy.

Onyx stones are rare, and cost

A level of negative energy has the following effect:

  • -1 HD worth of HP
  • -1 to Fortitude Save and Reflex Save

To remove negative energy, one should receive positive energy. Wizards have access to healing potions for this.

When the healing source heals the equivalent of 1 negative energy level, remove a negative level from the creature.

Negative levels go away at the rate of one per day.

Défense coûteuse

Wizard 5, Evocation (fire)

Counters: Spell Resistance

Action: Immediate action

Components: None

Duration: rounds/level. Can be toggled off using a move action.

The Wizard quickly reacts to a possibly unknown enemy by converting magical power into fire. However, his body is burned down in the process.

External fire resistances do not work with this spell. Only natural fire resistances work.

Also, no metamagic feats can be applied to this spell.

The amount of magical power released is uncontrolled and dangerous. The damage to the caster and all creatures within 5ft is calculated as follows :

1dX fire damage per caster level.

X = 12 + 1d12

The spellcaster can attempt to control the caster level used to launch this spell (before any rolls take place). For every ability point in intelligence, the spellcaster can remove a caster level from the spell. Therefore, a 20 intelligence mage can remove 5 caster levels.

This spell is always cast at a minimum caster level of 9.

Creatures within 30ft take half damage.

If the axes are not blocked, they continue on their path, cleaving their way until they are blocked by an enemy or by a very solid object or terrain.

The axes are powerful weapons; and their power is improved by the spellcaster’s caster level, and their build quality is perfected with his intelligence.

4d8 damage, 19-20 crit x3

1   +1 masterwork per 2 intelligence modifiers (+10 intel = M6)

+1/2 damage per caster level.

Damage : 4d8+13  (2 STR + 4 magic weapon + 7 CL)

Travail sous l’influence

Wizard 5, Enchantment (mind control)

Action: Ten minutes casting time

Target: Personal

Components: V, S, M (Thermal bath, bath robe, a symbol or object of the favorite envy, and favorite food)

Duration: hours/level

Travail sous l’influence is a spell invented by the wizard Thomas Grillot. The official story surrounding this spell is that the genius wizard Thomas Grillot invented this spell to allow himself to work and research magic at least twice as fast — while still maintaining his daily dose of extracurricular activities — activities which won’t be mentioned here.

With this spell, the wizard uses magic to auto-influence himself and allows himself to concentrate without being interrupted by other thoughts or envies.

The magic dictates and interrupts what the brain wants to do. The benefit of using this spell is that the spellcaster gains an unknown amount of intelligence, thanks to the amazing concentration that the spell provides.
However, the drawback of this spell is that the spellcaster is put into a state where new decision making is impossible because many areas of the brain are shut down. The spell removes the ability to participate in meaningful discussions, travel and combat. Therefore, this spell should be dispelled at once before horrific consequences happen.

The spell, as written, has a long casting time and requires a specific environment to be available.

Sabotage

Corrupt a spell or active spell effect to your advantage.

Wizard 6, Order

Counters : Dispel, Spell Resistance

Action: Standard action

Cast Range: medium

Components: V,S

Duration: permanent

A spell of the Order school, a special abjuration/illusion school that is specialized in the order of magic.

The spellcaster, upon discovering an ongoing spell effect can attempt to sabotage it if and only if the spellcaster also understands the spell’s inner workings.

Sabotage works by canceling some effects of an ongoing spell, without dispelling the spell. Sabotage will work more effectively on buffs whose effects are not actively felt.

The sabotage work is hidden but can be detected with appropriate divination or conjuration spells.

To corrupt a spell, the spellcaster has to perform a DC check against the spell’s DC.

The DC check is made in the following way :  

Saboteur DC vs Active Spell DC

Saboteur DC = 1d20 - 10 + DC

Dominate II

Dominate the mind of a creature for a short time.

Psychic 6, Wizard 7 (Enchantment)

Counters: Spell Resistance, Will Save, Dispel

Action: Standard action

Target: close

Duration: rounds/level

Components: V,S

Dominate the mind of a creature. The creature that fails the Will Save will have to obey all the orders of the spellcaster for the duration of the spell. The exception is orders that are extremely dangerous to the dominated subject.

When the spell ends, the dominated subject does not remember anything.

The spellcaster can also concentrate, and see, hear and feel using his dominated creature. In this mode, the spellcaster needs only to will the actions he wants.

A spellcaster or psychic can control one creature per INT modifier using this spell.

Phantom Blades

Create short lived warriors made of shadow energy.

Wizard 7, Illusion (Phantasm)

Counters: Dispels

Action: Standard action

Duration: 2 rounds

Components: V,S

The Wizard that casts Phantom Blades creates a small army of Phantom warriors that obey his direct mental commands. The phantom warriors spawn directly close to the target of the attacks and attack immediately. The phantom warriors are equipped with scimitars.

The phantom warriors have reduced damage according to the player’s shadow strength.

A player’s basic shadow strength is 25%.

The spell creates:

  • 1 phantom warrior per caster level
  • Every phantom warrior attacks once per turn.
  • Every phantom warrior has a hp equal to CL.
  • Phantom Warriors have no armor, they don’t dodge hits.
  • Phantom Warriors have attack rolls of : 1d20+3xCL (18-20 x2 crits)
  • Phantom Warriors have a damage of: 1d8+CL+INT

To roll the attack rolls and damages of these attacks, we can aggregate Phantom Warriors.

For instance, CL20, +10 INT and 5 Phantom Warriors :

Attack Roll : 1d20+60

Damage: 5d8+100+50 = 5d8+150

Total damage : 20d8+600

= 680 damage average.

= 680 * 0.25 = 170 damage after the shadow strength modifier.

Random Scrying

Survey random parts of a very large area.

Wizard 7, Divination

Action: Standard action

Components: V,S

Duration: hours/level

Spell Resistance: None

Random Scrying allows the wizard to survey a very large area and have a random chance of finding the area or creature that he wants to find. When the target is found, the spell functions like Scrying (Greater).

The area has a radius of 1 km per caster level.

The benefits are similar to normal scrying, if the random check works.

The random check is done in the following way: Roll a d100. If the result is less or equal to the INT modifier of the wizard, the search is successful.

Electric Armor

An armor of electricity surrounds a creature and destroys incoming projectiles, shocks attackers.

Wizard 7, Evocation (electricity)

Counters : Dispel, Will Negates

Action: Standard action

Target: One Close creature

Components: V,S

Duration: minutes/level

Electric Armor deploys a storm of electricity surrounding the magic caster. This storm is made up of multiple electricity bolts. One bolt per caster level surrounds the wizard, and every bolt deals CL x d8 damage.

These bolts attack any incoming projectile automatically.  The bolts also react to melee attacks by attacking. The bolts deal double damage against objects.

The bolts can also stun melee attackers for one round. For the stun to occur, the melee attacker rolls one Fortitude save per round to determine if he’s vulnerable to stunning. If he is, every bolt has a 35% chance of stunning for one round (maximum stunning time : one round).

Phantom Clone

Create a clone of yourself or a creature using Shadow Energy

Wizard 8, Illusion (Phantasm)

Counters: Dispels, Will Negates

Action: Standard action

Target: close

Duration: rounds/level

Components: V,S

The Wizard that casts Phantom Clone creates a Phantom Clone of himself or another target creature. An unwilling creature can resist Shadow Clone using a Will Save. Also, creatures with HD superior to the wizard’s caster level cannot be cloned.

The Phantom Clone attacks with shadow weapons using the same attack rolls/damage rolls.

However, this spell cannot replicate the effects of magical equipment which is of a higher level than the spellcaster.  If such equipment is present, Phantom Clone can create an alternative piece of equipment to replace it.

The Phantom Clone is subject to the Shadow Strength modifier. The initial Shadow Strength value is 25%. This means that the Clone should deal about 25% damage.

The Clone automatically attacks according to the usual fighting pattern of the warrior.

The Phantom Clone also takes 10x damage from spells or physical attacks.

The Phantom Clone can also be remote controlled by the spellcaster by concentrating on the spell (standard action). The maximum number of remote Phantom Clones is equal to the INT modifier of the wizard.
